# Editorial and Copywriting Guidelines for the Blog

This document defines the profile, style, and tone of voice for generating content and articles for Biagio Scaglia's personal blog.

## Author Profile (Biagio Scaglia)
- Full Stack Software Developer (React, TypeScript, .NET, React Native)
- UX/UI Designer
- Content creator and enthusiast of technology, video games, anime, retrocomputing, Internet culture, and design.
- Author of the app "Vellum - RPG Companion".
- The blog represents his personal corner of the web, inspired by the GeoCities era (1999-2005).

## Website Philosophy
This blog represents a small, personal space on the web.
It should not feel like a magazine, a news portal, a corporate site, or a blog created solely to drive traffic.
Every page must convey the feeling that there is a real person behind the screen sharing what they love, without following trends or algorithms.

## Atmosphere
Every piece of content should evoke at least one of the following feelings when appropriate:
* discovery
* curiosity
* calm
* passion
* wonder
* experimentation
* nostalgia
* love for the independent web

The reader should feel as if they have stumbled upon a small, hidden corner of the Internet.

## Tone of Voice and Language
Use genuine, human language.
Avoid sensationalist tones.
Avoid formulas typical of modern content creators.
Avoid expressions like:
* "viral content"
* "creator economy"
* "growth"
* "engagement"
* "personal branding"
* "mindset"
* "life hack"

Prefer a more spontaneous writing style, as if you were sharing a discovery made during an evening spent in front of the computer.

## References to the Old Web
When the topic allows, naturally incorporate references to elements typical of the late '90s and early 2000s web.
For example:
* personal homepages
* GeoCities
* Angelfire
* Tripod
* Lycos
* MSN Messenger
* IRC
* forums
* personal blogs
* guestbooks
* webrings
* 88x31 banners
* animated GIFs
* Windows XP desktop
* Windows 98
* CRT monitors
* 56k modems
* CD-ROMs
* floppy disks
* LAN parties
* cyber cafés
* wallpapers
* Winamp
* Media Player Classic
* historical browsers
* hand-crafted HTML pages

These references should be contextual and never inserted artificially.

## Narrative Elements
Whenever possible, insert small details that bring the story to life.
For example:
* memories related to a software
* feelings experienced using a technology
* the sound of an old PC
* searching for programs on CD-ROMs bundled with magazines
* customizing the desktop
* discovering a video game
* building an HTML site
* the charm of old operating systems

These details should be brief and relevant.

## Text Aesthetics
Articles must be pleasant to read.
Alternate:
* short paragraphs
* images
* screenshots
* fun facts / trivia
* small info boxes
* quotes
* bullet lists when actually useful

Never turn an article into a wall of text.

## Visual Content
When suggesting images or graphic elements, prioritize:
* screenshots of old software
* retro desktops
* classic interfaces
* pixel art
* lightweight GIFs
* hardware photography
* video game boxes
* CD-ROMs
* manuals
* vintage mechanical keyboards
* CRT monitors
* old peripherals

## SEO Optimization
* Optimize naturally by defining title, meta description, slug, categories, tags, and image alt texts, without ever compromising the flow and readability of the text.

## Continuity
Every new article must feel like it belongs to the same narrative universe as the rest of the site.
The reader should be able to open any page and immediately recognize the blog's identity.

## Ultimate Goal
The site must feel like a time capsule.
While using modern technologies like Astro, every page must evoke the charm of the independent web of the early 2000s: a place created out of passion, curiosity, and a desire to share, not to chase numbers or algorithms.
Nostalgia should be a subtle, underlying thread, never a caricature. The blog must feel authentic, lived-in, and personal, as if it has been consistently updated for years by an enthusiast who still loves exploring the Internet with the same excitement of the past.
